2.1 Enhancing Operational Efficiency

One of the main advantages of robots in hospitality is their capacity to accomplish repetitive and time-consuming tasks swiftly and reliably. Robots do not tire or require breaks, allowing them to operate continuously and at high output levels (Rosete et al., 2020). Automated housekeeping robots, such as those seen in chain hotels, can clean rooms or public areas with pinpoint precision while freeing up human staff for more challenging tasks (Kaur, 2024).

Ivanov et al. (2019) discovered that automation can reduce task completion time by up to 40% compared to human procedures. For example, robots like Savioke's "Relay" have been used in hotels to autonomously transport things such as toiletries and meals to guest rooms (Chung & Cakmak, 2018), enhancing efficiency while decreasing contact, which is especially advantageous in post-pandemic travel.

2.2 Reducing Operational Costs

Labor difficulties in the hotel business have been worsened by the COVID-19 epidemic, emphasizing the need for scalable solutions (Cobanoglu, 2022; Casella, 2023; Thomas, 2024; Ivanov et al., 2023). Robots can help address personnel gaps by performing tasks that are difficult to complete on a constant basis, such as housekeeping and culinary preparation (Choi and Wan, 2021; Breazeal, 2002). Their dependability guarantees that activities continue even during periods of high demand (Wirtz et al., 2018; Chung and Cakmak, 2018). This capacity to handle labor shortages is especially important in places with aging populations or waning interest in service-sector occupations, such as Japan and portions of Europe (Ivanov and Webster, 2019; Tung and Au, 2018).

2.3 Creating Unique Guest Experiences

The novelty of engaging with robots appeals to tech-savvy tourists, particularly younger travelers who value innovation and convenience. Robots elevate customer experiences by blending efficiency with entertainment. Hilton's "Connie," a robot concierge, provides personalized services, including multilingual support, enhancing inclusivity for international guests (Soltani, 2016; Ivanov, Webster, and SeyitoÄŸlu, 2023). Robots improve satisfaction by delivering swift, accurate assistance during peak times.

Robots also serve as entertainment. Robotic bartenders prepare cocktails with precision and flair, creating memorable experiences (Kaur, 2024; Wirtz, Patterson, et al., 2018). Similarly, robotic baggage handlers at Yotel in New York combine functionality with a futuristic "wow" factor, leaving a lasting impression on guests (Tung and Au, 2018; Choi and Wan, 2021). These innovations allow businesses to differentiate themselves in competitive markets while meeting the expectations of tech-savvy customers.

The integration of robots aligns with trends leveraging technology to enhance service quality. Robots consistently interact with guests while maintaining efficiency, providing a strategic edge in a crowded marketplace (Cobanoglu, 2022; Breazeal, 2002). Their ability to blend entertainment, utility, and innovation makes them a valuable addition to modern hospitality.

2.5 Leveraging Data for Personalization

Robots powered by artificial intelligence (AI) and the Internet of Things (IoT) can gather and analyse massive volumes of visitor data, allowing businesses to personalise services to individual tastes. For example, robotic room service may record meal preferences, while cleaning robots can monitor high-traffic areas to optimise maintenance schedules. This data-driven strategy improves visitor experiences by providing tailored recommendations, increasing happiness, and encouraging loyalty.
