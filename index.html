Conclusion

Integrating robotics into the hotel industry offers both opportunities and challenges. Robots enhance efficiency, cost savings, and guest experiences but raise concerns about job displacement, reduced personal interaction, and ethical issues. A balanced approach is vital to maximise their potential while preserving hospitalityâ€™s human-centric values. Businesses should focus on inclusivity, accessibility, sustainability, and enhancing the guest experience. Addressing employee concerns through training and communication is key to maintaining morale.

Acceptance of service robots depends on functionality, ease of use, and their ability to deliver social-emotional and relational benefits (Wirtz et al., 2018, 2022). Consumer acceptance of service robots is strongly influenced by their human-like traits, such as appearance and engaging behaviours (e.g., smiling) (Breazeal, 2003). Trust increases when consumers feel safe and comfortable with the technology, aided by emotional expressions and familiar, comforting designs (Wirtz et al., 2018). Robots that meet social-emotional and relational needs, akin to human staff, play a key role in acceptance.

As technology advances, robots will create new opportunities, allowing the hospitality industry to redefine service excellence through collaboration between humans and machines.
