Drawback

3.1 High Initial and Maintenance Costs

While robots provide long-term benefits, their initial expenses provide a considerable obstacle to implementation. Purchasing, installing, and integrating robotic systems into existing infrastructure requires a significant investment (Loiola, 2023). Furthermore, continual maintenance, software upgrades, and repairs can strain operating resources, especially for small and medium-sized businesses (SMEs). For example, if a robotic concierge fails, rapid technical assistance is required, potentially resulting in downtime and service disruptions. For enterprises with low profit margins, these expenses may exceed the advantages.

3.2 Loss of Human Touch

The hospitality industry thrives on human-centric service, with personal interactions and emotional intelligence being pivotal to guest satisfaction. Despite advancements in robotics, machines cannot replicate human empathy or address emotionally nuanced guest concerns effectively. For example, an automated concierge may fail to comfort a distressed traveler or manage complex issues appropriately.

3.3 Technical Challenges and Cybersecurity Risks

Robotic systems are susceptible to technological failures and cybersecurity risks. Malfunctions during peak service hours disrupt operations and negatively impact guest experiences (Chung & Cakmak, 2018; Wirtz et al., 2022). Additionally, as robots collect and process guest data, they become targets for cyberattacks. Ensuring robust security measures, such as encryption and regular audits, is essential to protect sensitive information and maintain trust (Andrew, 1984; Casella, 2023). Failure to address these risks can result in data breaches, reputational damage, and regulatory penalties (Bulchand-Gidumal et al., 2023; Özşeker, et al., 2022).

3.4 Job Displacement and Socio-Economic Implications

The growing adoption of robotics raises ethical concerns about job displacement and socio-economic inequality. Automation reduces the need for human labor, particularly in entry-level roles, potentially increasing unemployment and lowering staff morale (Thomas, 2024; Kaur, 2024). For example, replacing housekeeping staff with robotic cleaners could provoke criticism from the local community and negatively impact employee livelihoods (Loiola, 2023; Ivanov, et al., 2023).

3.5 Limited Acceptance Across Demographics

While younger, tech-savvy travelers may embrace robotics, older generations and culturally conservative markets may resist such advancements. In regions where personal service is highly valued, such as parts of India and the Middle East, robotic solutions may be perceived as impersonal or culturally inappropriate (Rosete et al., 2020; Palrão et al., 2023). Understanding these demographic and cultural differences is critical for businesses aiming to implement robotic technologies without alienating key customer segments (Wirtz et al., 2018; Tung & Au, 2018).
